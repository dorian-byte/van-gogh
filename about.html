ABOUT VINCENT VAN GOGH

(1853 - 1890)

A Dutch post-impressionist painter renowned for his vivid colors and emotive brushwork.

Though he faced mental health struggles and remained largely unrecognized during his lifetime, his work later gained immense fame, influencing 20th-century art. Iconic pieces like "Starry Night" and "Sunflowers" showcase his innovative style and deep emotional expression. Van Gogh's tragic life ended in suicide, but his legacy endures, celebrated for its passion, intensity, and groundbreaking approach to color and form.
